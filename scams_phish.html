Guide for Spotting a Phish!

Phishing goes beyond sketchy emails. Watch out:

Phishing is a major cyber threat for college students. It exploits human psychology, not tech flaws. Attackers create urgency or curiosity to trick you. One click can compromise your data. This is an entire business model, not a prank.

This guide helps spot traps quickly.

  • Fake School Alerts: Emails from Canvas or IT asking for your password on a fake login page.
  • QR Code Traps (Quishing): Malicious codes on flyers promising free deals that lead to bad sites. Remote students see these in emails or public places.
  • Text or Voice Scams (Smishing/Vishing): Messages requesting your MFA code, often impersonating a bank or the university.

The Simple Tricks Behind Phishing

Attackers use low-tech deception.

  • Link Masking: The text says "Official Site," but the actual link is malicious.
    • Tip: Hover your mouse to preview its real destination.
  • Lookalike Domains: Attackers use similar domains like microscft-login.com.
    • Tip: Always double-check the real domain name.

Quick Safety Checklist for Students

Reduce risk by following steps:

  • Preview links before you click.
  • Avoid scanning random QR codes; type the URL.
  • Turn on MFA for all accounts. It is crucial for security.
  • Verify urgent requests via an official channel.
  • Use strong, unique passwords. (A password manager is helpful).
  • Keep your devices updated. Run antivirus scans.

If You Fall for a Phishing Attempt

act immediately if you click a bad link.

  1. Disconnect from Wi-Fi or internet fast.
  2. Change the password on another secure device.
  3. Enable or confirm MFA is active.
  4. Report it: Forward the message to IT/security.
  5. Monitor accounts for unusual activity.

Final Tip: When in Doubt, Pause

Attackers want you rushed. A five-second check saves you time. Security starts with awareness, even when studying remotely.
